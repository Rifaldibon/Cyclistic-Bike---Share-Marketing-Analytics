# 📊 CYCLISTIC BOARDROOM PRESENTATION
**Subject:** Converting Casual Riders to Annual Members Through Behavioral Insights  
**Role:** Junior Data Analyst, Marketing Analytics Team  
**Company:** Cyclistic Bike-Share (Chicago)  
**Case Study:** Google Data Analytics Capstone Project  

---

## 🎯 1. The Business Task
To design a data-driven marketing strategy aimed at **converting casual riders into annual members** by understanding exactly how their bike usage behavior differs, thereby securing predictable, long-term revenue growth for Cyclistic.

---

## 🔑 2. Compelling Data Insights (Backed by 5.3M Clean Trips)
To secure executive approval, our arguments must rely heavily on empirical data. Here are the three pillars of behavioral differences discovered in our analysis:

### 📈 Insight 1: Utilitarian Commuters vs. Leisure Explorers (Temporal Behavior)
* **The Data:** Annual Members peak heavily on **Tuesdays (584,595 trips)** and maintain high, stable volume Monday through Thursday, dropping drastically on weekends. Casual riders show the exact opposite trend—their usage is low during the workweek but spikes **3x higher on Saturday (361,154 trips)** compared to Monday.
* **Executive Takeaway:** Members use Cyclistic out of **necessity** (daily work/school commuting), while Casuals use it for **leisure and lifestyle** (weekend recreation).

### 🗺️ Insight 2: Corporate Transit Hubs vs. Waterfront Tourism (Spatial Behavior)
* **The Data:** The top station for Members is **Canal St & Adams St (44,337 trips)**, located directly outside Union Station (a major commuter train hub). Conversely, the absolute top hotspot for Casual riders is **Navy Pier (40,273 trips)**, flanked heavily by scenic lakeside paths like DuSable Lake Shore Drive.
* **Executive Takeaway:** We cannot pitch a *"save time on your daily commute"* marketing narrative to casual riders hanging out at tourist destinations. Marketing placements must be geographically customized.

### ⚡ Insight 3: Premium Technology Adoption & High Willingness-to-Pay (Product Behavior)
* **The Data:** Casual riders have a longer average ride duration every single day of the week, and a striking **72.45% of Casual trips are made on Electric Bikes** (which incur higher per-minute costs), compared to 66.32% for Members.
* **Executive Takeaway:** Casual riders are highly willing to spend extra money for convenience, speed, and comfort. They haven't bought memberships yet simply because our current membership tiers do not cater to their recreational habits.

---

## 💡 3. Data-Driven Marketing Recommendations for Executive Approval
Based on these insights, here are three actionable conversion strategies designed to turn Casual "Leisure Explorers" into Annual "Weekend Warriors":

### 🟢 Strategy 1: Introduce the "Cyclistic Weekend Warrior" Annual Membership
* **The Problem:** Casual riders reject standard annual memberships because they know they won't use the bikes on weekdays.
* **The Solution:** Launch a new, lower-tier annual membership option specifically tailored for leisure riders. This pass offers unlimited free or heavily discounted unlocking privileges exclusively from **Friday afternoon through Sunday**.

### 🟢 Strategy 2: Geo-Targeted Digital Campaigns at Tourism Hotspots
* **The Problem:** Blasting mass-marketing campaigns across all of Chicago is expensive and inefficient.
* **The Solution:** Deploy high-intent digital ads (Instagram/Google geofencing) and digital billboards active *only* around **Navy Pier, DuSable Lake Shore Dr, and Theater on the Lake**. The ad narrative should focus on cost-savings: *"Visited Navy Pier twice this month? Save $XX by switching to our new Weekend Annual Pass!"*

### 🟢 Strategy 3: "Member-Only" E-Bike Priority Booking
* **The Problem:** Casual riders are obsessed with Electric Bikes (72.45% choice rate), leading to high competition and empty docks at popular lakeside stations on weekends.
* **The Solution:** Introduce a premium app feature allowing Annual Members to **reserve an E-bike 10 minutes in advance** at high-density stations. This creates an exclusive perk that pushes frustrated casual tourists to purchase a membership just to secure an E-bike during busy weekend hours.
